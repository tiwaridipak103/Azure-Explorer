2️⃣ Understanding the Response: vacancy
Definition: Vacancy represents the proportion of unoccupied real estate space (could be office, industrial, retail, or multifamily housing) in the market.

Economic Interpretation:

Higher employment → higher demand → lower vacancy (in many cases, especially industrial or office space).

Lower employment → lower demand → higher vacancy.

Quarterly Dynamics: Vacancies tend to respond with a lag to employment changes:

Immediate effects are small.

Over 1–4 quarters, changes in employment can cause noticeable shifts in vacancy.

Lagged effect	Employment changes affect vacancy after a quarter or two.
Non-linear effects	Sometimes, a small increase in jobs doesn’t affect vacancy, but beyond a threshold, it starts reducing vacancy noticeably.
Market-dependent	Some markets may be more sensitive to manufacturing employment than others. For example, Detroit may be more sensitive than Austin.
