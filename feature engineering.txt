1️⃣ What is construction_cost_index (CCI)?
The Construction Cost Index (CCI) measures the relative cost of construction over time.

It usually combines materials, labor, and equipment costs required to construct new buildings.

A higher CCI → construction is more expensive; a lower CCI → construction is cheaper.

Since your dataset is quarterly, the index likely changes every quarter reflecting cost trends.

Important: The CCI doesn’t tell you about the number of buildings being constructed directly — it’s about cost per unit of construction, not the volume.

2️⃣ How does construction_cost_index impact vacancy?
Vacancy refers to the percentage of unoccupied real estate space in a market.

The connection is nuanced:

Concept	Mechanism	Effect
High CCI	Construction becomes more expensive	Developers may delay or reduce new construction, limiting supply
Reduced Supply	Fewer units enter the market	Vacancy may fall if demand remains stable
Low CCI	Construction is cheaper	More projects start
Additional notes:

The effect is lagged. A spike in CCI today might affect vacancy 2–4 quarters later, because construction takes time.

The relationship is nonlinear. Extremely high CCI may deter construction entirely, while moderate changes may have minimal effect.

Market type matters. For example:

Multifamily apartments: sensitive to material/labor cost increases.

Industrial warehouses: may be more sensitive to demand trends than construction costs alone.

In slow markets, small rises in CCI can significantly delay projects, reducing supply, and lowering vacancy.

This means CCI-vacancy relationship can differ across markets, which is important for Moody’s multi-market dataset.
