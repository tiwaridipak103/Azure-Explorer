1. What construction_employment is
construction_employment represents the number of people employed in the construction sector in a specific market during a given quarter.

It can indicate construction activity levels — when more people are employed in construction, typically more projects are underway (residential, commercial, or infrastructure).

Why it matters for real estate:

Higher construction employment → more ongoing construction → potentially more future supply of properties.

Lower construction employment → slower construction → supply growth may be limited.

2. How it can relate to vacancy
vacancy usually measures the proportion of available real estate units that are unoccupied.

The relationship could be interpreted as:

Scenario	Construction Employment Trend	Expected Vacancy Impact	Reasoning
Increasing construction employment	High	Vacancy might increase later	More units being built → supply increases → some units remain unoccupied initially.
Decreasing construction employment	Low	Vacancy might decrease or stay stable	Fewer units being built → slower supply growth → existing units more likely to be occupied.
Note: The effect is lagged. For example, people employed in construction today affect vacancy 6–12 months later, once the buildings are completed and leased or sold.

Quarter-over-Quarter (QoQ) Change:

construction_employment_diff = construction_employment_this_quarter - construction_employment_last_quarter
Year-over-Year (YoY) Change:
