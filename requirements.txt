🧠 Step 1: First — Define “Customer Activity” (Critical)
In retail, RFM = purchases.
In your case, you must define what counts as activity:

Meetings (engagement)

💡 Best approach: Build MULTIPLE RFM variants, then combine.
🧱 Step 2: Core RFM Feature Sets
🔵 A. Engagement RFM (Meetings-based)
🟢 Recency (R₁)
days_since_last_meeting
days_since_last_strategic_meeting (if strategies discussed > 0)
🔵 Frequency (F₁)
total_meetings_last_3m
total_meetings_last_6m
total_meetings_last_12m
💡 Better:
meeting_frequency_trend = (last_3m / last_6m)
🟡 Monetary (M₁) → proxy
Since meetings don’t have money:
total_strategies_discussed
avg_strategies_per_meeting
👉 This acts as depth of engagement
🔵 B. Financial RFM (AUM-based) — MOST IMPORTANT
🟢 Recency (R₂)
days_since_last_cash_flow
days_since_last_positive_flow (contribution)
🔵 Frequency (F₂)
num_cash_flow_events_last_12m
num_contributions_last_12m
num_withdrawals_last_12m
🟡 Monetary (M₂)
total_net_flow_12m = contributions - withdrawals
avg_monthly_aum
aum_growth_rate
💡 Strong feature:
volatility_of_aum (std dev of AUM)
🔵 C. Opportunity RFM (Pipeline-based)
🟢 Recency (R₃)
days_since_last_opportunity_created

🔵 Frequency (F₃)

🧮 Step 4: Aggregation Strategy (VERY IMPORTANT)
Since your data is multi-level:
👉 Final dataset should be at Company level
So:
Aggregate Financial Account → Company
Aggregate AUM → Financial Account → Company
Aggregate Meetings → Company
💡 Use:
SUM → monetary
COUNT → frequency
MAX/MIN → recency

❌ Not normalizing skewed features (log1p is your friend 😉)
